## Trip Summary

This itinerary contains a sanitized 13-day Austria travel plan covering Vienna, Salzburg, Obertraun / Hallstatt, Innsbruck, and a final return to Vienna.

It is designed for testing the Holiday Planner project and preparing for future RAG usage.

The itinerary prioritizes iconic landmarks, cafes, museums, scenic walks, city-pass usage, food stops, public transport efficiency, and practical travel-day routing.

## Day 1

Theme: Vienna arrival and classic old town introduction

Morning:
- Travel day
- Arrive in Vienna in the afternoon

Afternoon:
- Check in and settle near the accommodation area
- Travel to Stephansplatz
- Visit St. Stephen’s Cathedral exterior and surrounding square
- Walk through Graben, Kohlmarkt, Hoher Markt, and Ankeruhr

Evening:
- Visit Peterskirche area
- Have dinner in Vienna’s old centre
- Return to accommodation by public transport

Food:
- Optional arrival snack near accommodation or main station
- Suggested snack options: bakery sandwich, pastry, light station snack
- Dinner options: Figlmüller, Lugeck, or Plachutta
- Recommended order: schnitzel with potato salad, goulash, or Tafelspitz

Transport:
- Use Vienna public transport
- U-Bahn is the main route into the historic centre
- Airport transfer is separate unless an airport add-on is purchased

Notes:
- St. Stephen’s Cathedral exterior is free
- Towers, catacombs, and treasury are paid
- Peterskirche is free to enter
- Concerts are optional and may require payment or donation

## General Austria Tips

- Use city cards mainly for local public transport first, and attraction discounts or free entries second.
- Major Vienna attractions are usually not free with the Vienna City Card.
- Salzburg Card and Innsbruck Card provide stronger attraction value than Vienna City Card.
- Book timed attractions in advance where possible.
- Check opening hours close to travel dates, especially for 2026 renovation or seasonal attractions.
- Keep cafe and coffee stops between 08:00 and 16:00.
- Use accommodation areas as route anchors to avoid unnecessary transport interchanges.
- For mountain days, check weather, cable car operation, and last descent times.
- For Hallstatt and Obertraun, check evening transport and restaurant availability in advance.
- Intercity trains are separate from city cards.
- Carry some cash for traditional beer halls, mountain huts, and smaller village stops.
- Use live transport apps for Hallstatt, Obertraun, and Dachstein connections.
